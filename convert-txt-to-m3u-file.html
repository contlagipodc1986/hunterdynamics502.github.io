I have received many messages from users who can't add .txt channel lists in the XBMC or KODI. First of all, you cannot send a file with the .txt format, because the equipment does not recognize. What you can and should do, is to convert the .txt file to .m3u, and then upload manually to the XBMC or KODI.

At the request of some users, I decided to create a tutorial that shows step by step how to convert our .txt files to .m3u.

Before we start the tutorial, please, download Notepad .

After installation of the Notepad , follow these steps below.

1 – Choose one of the available lists and select download. The Music list is just an example for the tutorial.

2 – Open the .txt file, select and copy the link of the pastebin.

3 – Open the browser, paste the link and then press the Enter button on your keyboard.

4 – After the download is complete, move the mouse arrow to the top of the .txt file, click the right mouse button and select Edit with Notepad .

5 – Then will appear the list through the Notepad , and to convert the .txt file, select File>>Save as.

6 – Now pay close attention. Change the name of the file to “example.m3u” and then select Save. In my case was to “music.m3u“. You can give any name you like to your list, since the end of the word always contains .m3u. This is a very simple process!

7 – As shown in the image, the new file with .m3u format is created. Now all you have to do is send the file to the XBMC or KODI. I hope that you have understood our tutorial. Enjoy it.
